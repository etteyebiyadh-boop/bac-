# BacLang — 10-Slide Pitch Outline

Use for investors, accelerators, or school partnerships. One idea per slide.

---

### Slide 1 — Title
- **BacLang** — Language prep that scores like the exam.
- Tagline: *Train like the bac. Improve in minutes.*
- Logo + contact.

### Slide 2 — Problem
- Bac language sections are **high-stakes**; students juggle grammar, essays, and exam format under time pressure.
- Generic apps teach vocabulary; **they don’t optimize for /20 writing and exam prompts.**

### Slide 3 — Solution
- **Instant bac-style correction**: score /20, rubric breakdown, corrected text, next steps.
- **Structured practice**: curated prompts, methodology hints, model answers.
- **Habit loop**: dashboard, streaks, daily missions, lesson library (roadmap-aligned).

### Slide 4 — Product (screens / flow)
- Write → score + breakdown → recommended lesson → next exam prompt.
- Show one mobile screenshot mock (hero + result panel).

### Slide 5 — Why now
- AI quality + structured JSON → **reliable** feedback at scale.
- Students already study on phones; **mobile-first** is default.

### Slide 6 — Business model
- **Freemium**: limited free corrections per week; full archive + unlimited corrections + more languages on **Premium**.
- **B2C first**; B2B (schools) later.

### Slide 7 — Traction (fill with real numbers)
- Waitlist, beta users, retention, NPS, sample score improvement.
- *Placeholder until you have data.*

### Slide 8 — Moat
- **Rubric + pedagogy** tuned to Tunisian bac (not generic English).
- **Data flywheel**: submissions → weakest skill → lessons → retention.
- **Brand trust** with students and teachers in Tunisia.

### Slide 9 — Roadmap
- **Now:** English MVP, exams, correction, missions, lessons shell.
- **Next:** payments, French/Arabic, richer content.
- **Later:** institutions, optional languages, speaking (optional).

### Slide 10 — Ask
- What you need: **funding amount**, use of funds (content, marketing, infra), **team**.
- Clear CTA: pilot schools or angel meeting.

---

**Design tip:** Slide 4 and 7 should be the most visual; keep slides 2–3 text-light.
